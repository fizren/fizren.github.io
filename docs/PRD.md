# Product Requirements Document

## Metadata

* **Product name:** Piotr Statkiewicz Personal Website
* **Version:** 0.1
* **Status:** Draft
* **Last updated:** 2026-03-15

## Executive Summary

A personal website and private project for publishing notes, photographs, family history writing, professional information, and a public reference page about Małolat (Zbigniew Czyżewski). The site is intended to be calm, readable, and easy to maintain over time, with simple navigation and a small number of reusable content patterns.

## Problem Statement

Piotr needs one stable place to publish things that matter to him without depending on fragmented platforms or social-media-style publishing models. The content is varied, but it shares a common purpose: personal expression, documentation, and long-term preservation.

The challenge is not how to publish everything everywhere. The challenge is to make different kinds of material feel coherent within one personal website while keeping the workflow simple and sustainable.

## Solution Overview

The product is a minimal personal publishing and archive website organized into five sections:

* Notes
* Photos
* Work
* Family
* Małolat

The homepage explains the project and lets visitors move quickly to the section they want. Each section has a clear editorial role and a consistent format.

Notes and photos are published as new entries over time. Family content is published as a small set of living essays that can be updated as research evolves. Work and Małolat act as stable reference pages.

The site favors reading clarity, visual calm, and low-friction publishing over feature richness.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

Piotr Statkiewicz as author, editor, and maintainer.

### Secondary Users (if any)

Friends, colleagues, and readers interested in Piotr’s notes or photography.

People looking for information about Małolat (Zbigniew Czyżewski).

Family members or interested readers exploring the family history essays.

## Goals and Success Signals

### Goals

Create a personal website that clearly presents itself as a private project.

Make it easy to publish and maintain content over time.

Give each section a distinct purpose without making the site feel fragmented.

Support family history writing as narrative, evolving essays rather than a tree or database.

Provide a reliable public reference page about Małolat (Zbigniew Czyżewski).

### Success Signals

A first-time visitor quickly understands what the website is and where to go next.

Piotr can publish new material without friction or hesitation.

Notes, photos, family essays, work, and Małolat content each feel natural in their section.

The site remains coherent as content grows slowly over time.

Family essays can be updated while keeping visible, readable research progress.

## Non-Goals

A social platform.

A portfolio optimized for self-promotion.

A full genealogy database or published family tree.

An academic archival system.

A large-scale photography platform.

A collaborative or community-edited website.

## Assumptions

The website will grow slowly through deliberate publishing rather than frequent posting.

Visitors will often arrive with one of two intents: understand Piotr’s personal project, or find a specific kind of content.

Narrative writing is more meaningful for the family section than systematic genealogical completeness.

The family section will be more valuable as a small set of evolving essays than as many short articles.

A minimal design with a few reusable patterns will be easier to maintain and more consistent with the tone of the project.

The author is comfortable maintaining the site through a repository-based workflow.

## Risks

The site may feel scattered if the homepage and section descriptions are too vague.

The family section may grow into very long essays that become harder to navigate.

The distinction between Notes and Family may blur if both become reflective long-form writing.

The Małolat page may attract visitors with a narrow information need who do not care about the rest of the site.

If content formats are not defined early, the site may become inconsistent and harder to maintain.

If filters or tags are introduced without restraint, navigation may become noisy rather than useful.

## Constraints

The site should remain simple to maintain.

Publishing should stay compatible with a lightweight repository-based workflow.

The design should remain minimal and avoid unnecessary page variants.

The homepage should orient visitors rather than promote a single section.

No section needs aggressive growth or frequent updates to justify its existence.

## Phase 1

### Intended Outcome

A minimal but real first version of the website exists and already expresses the full concept of the project.

### What Exists After This Phase

A homepage with a short explanation of the project.

Clear navigation to Notes, Photos, Work, Family, and Małolat.

At least one representative piece of content in the sections that need it.

A stable reading experience that feels calm and intentional.

A first version of the Małolat page that already serves as a reliable reference.

### New User Capability

Visitors can understand what the website is, choose a section quickly, and read or view content without confusion.

Piotr can see the website as a real publishing home rather than a placeholder.

### Validation Focus

Does the site already feel like one coherent personal project?

Can a first-time visitor understand the structure immediately?

Does the homepage explain enough without over-explaining?

### Failure Signal

The site still feels like a collection of unfinished pages rather than a deliberate whole.

## Phase 2

### Intended Outcome

Each section develops a consistent editorial identity and a repeatable publishing pattern.

### What Exists After This Phase

Notes follow a chronological pattern with optional topic navigation.

Photos are published as small connected series rather than isolated uploads.

Work functions as a stable professional reference section.

Family becomes a small collection of living essays with visible research updates.

Małolat becomes a clearer reference page with stronger structure for information, sources, and materials.

### New User Capability

Visitors can understand what kind of content to expect in each section.

Piotr can publish new content by following a small set of clear patterns rather than inventing structure each time.

### Validation Focus

Does each section now feel like it has its own job?

Can new material be added without creating new layout or content ambiguity?

Do family updates remain readable and meaningful to visitors?

### Failure Signal

Publishing a new item requires case-by-case decisions about structure, format, or placement.

## Phase 3

### Intended Outcome

The website becomes a sustainable long-term personal archive that can grow without losing clarity.

### What Exists After This Phase

A body of notes published over time.

A growing set of photo series.

A small but meaningful collection of family essays that have evolved through visible research updates.

A durable work section and a reliable public Małolat reference page.

Lightweight discovery paths such as topics, years, or related reading where useful.

### New User Capability

Visitors can browse older material with confidence and still understand how the site is organized.

Piotr can continue growing the archive without feeling pressure to redesign the site.

### Validation Focus

Does the site still feel calm and understandable after substantial growth?

Do the original section boundaries still help rather than constrain?

Can the project continue without needing a structural reset?

### Failure Signal

Growth makes the website feel cluttered, repetitive, or directionless.

## Open Questions

Should the Work section remain strictly a CV section, or should it leave room for future professional writing or projects?

Should the Family section be presented under the label “Family,” “Family History,” or another phrase closer to its editorial tone?

Should photo series be shown as stacked images, a light carousel, or both depending on context?

How visible should tags or topics be in Notes?

How visible should research updates be in Family essays: always expanded, or present but visually quiet?

Should the homepage include recent updates, or remain purely structural and timeless?
