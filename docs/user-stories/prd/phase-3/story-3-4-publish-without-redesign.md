# Story 3.4: Continue publishing for years without structural resets

**Priority**: Must Have  
**Dependencies**: Story 2.1, Story 2.3, Story 2.5  
**Estimated Complexity**: Medium  
**INVEST Check**: ✓ Independent, ✓ Negotiable, ✓ Valuable, ✓ Estimable, ✓ Small, ✓ Testable

**As a** Piotr (author/maintainer)  
**I want to** keep adding content with existing patterns year after year  
**So that** I can sustain the project without recurring redesign pressure

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Author adds new yearly content with no new template type
**Given** I publish one new note, one photo series, and one Family update in 2027  
**When** I use the existing section patterns  
**Then** each item fits naturally into its section  
**And** I do not need to invent a new layout or content class

### Scenario 2: Author keeps maintenance time predictable
**Given** I prepare monthly updates  
**When** I follow the established publishing flow  
**Then** publishing remains routine and low-friction  
**And** I do not face repeated structural decisions per item

### Scenario 3: Growth does not force major reorganization
**Given** the archive has grown substantially over multiple years  
**When** I plan new content  
**Then** I can continue within existing section boundaries  
**And** I do not need a site-wide IA reset to keep publishing

## Technical Notes

- Optimize for repeatable author workflow and low maintenance.
- Favor incremental improvements over redesign cycles.
- Keep content model simple enough for repository-based editing.

## Validation

- [ ] New yearly content can be published with existing patterns.
- [ ] Publishing does not require recurring structural redesign.
- [ ] Maintenance remains manageable for a single maintainer.
- [ ] Site still aligns with PRD constraints and non-goals.
